Red Fruits Cake

Fruit cakes are common in many cultures, often prepared according to seasonal fruits. Red fruits, rich in color and flavor, are widely used in desserts in Europe and North America. This cake is inspired by traditional recipes that use berries to create a balance between sweetness and acidity.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Flour
  • 1 packet baking powder
  • 150 g sugar
  • 100 ml vegetable oil or melted butter
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 120 ml milk
  • 200 g red fruits (raspberries, blueberries, chopped strawberries, currants)
  • 1 tablespoon powdered sugar for dusting (optional)
  • A pinch of salt

Steps:

  1. Prepare the batter: Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). In a bowl, beat the eggs with the sugar until light and fluffy. Add the oil or melted butter and vanilla extract, and mix well.
  2. Incorporate the dry ingredients: In another bowl, mix the flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add this mixture to the egg mixture, alternating with the milk, to obtain a smooth batter.
  3. Add the fruits: Gently fold the red fruits into the batter to avoid crushing them.
  4. Bake: Pour the batter into a greased and floured pan. Bake for 40 to 45 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean. Let it cool before removing from the pan, then dust with powdered sugar if desired.
  5. How to Store: Store the cake in the refrigerator in an airtight container to prevent the fruits from making the batter soggy.
